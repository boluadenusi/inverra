The 2022 FIFA World Cup was held in Qatar, marking the first time the tournament was hosted in the Middle East. Argentina won the championship, defeating France in what many consider the greatest World Cup final ever played.

Lionel Messi led Argentina to victory, finally winning the one trophy that had eluded him throughout his career. The final ended in a dramatic three-three draw after extra time, with Argentina winning on penalties.

Messi scored seven goals throughout the tournament and was awarded the Golden Ball as the best player. Kylian Mbappe of France won the Golden Boot with eight goals, including a hat-trick in the final.

The tournament was controversial due to concerns about human rights and the decision to hold it during winter rather than the traditional summer schedule. Despite the controversy, the football itself was spectacular.
